Backflow, also known as Backpressure or Back-Siphonage, is the unwanted flow of contaminated water in the reverse.
direction that can cause contamination of clean water lines. When measured on the pH scale, anything above a 7 on the
pH scale is considered a base. When the pH levels reach or exceed 12.5 pH, it then becomes hazardous. As with all hazardous
wastes, when in contact with human or environmental health, it can be very dangerous.
